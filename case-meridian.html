01

Discovery & Alignment

Every project starts with radical honesty. Meridian's brief was to build a recommendations engine — but research revealed that was the wrong problem entirely.

Problem Reframe
Original Brief
Better recommendations engine
→
Actual Problem
Personal trip canvas to hold research together
The "Five Whys" Framework

The diary study was the most valuable input. Watching how people actually plan — the tabs they keep open, the Instagram saves they reference — showed that nobody needed a better list. They needed a way to make sense of their existing research.

I ran 11 interviews with frequent independent travellers, a two-week diary study with 6 participants planning real trips, and a competitive audit of 14 platforms including Google Trips, TripAdvisor, and Notion-based templates.

The diary study shifted the product strategy entirely: from a curated recommendations engine to a personal trip canvas where users collect, organise, and sequence content alongside their own notes.

This reframe was a hard conversation with the co-founders — it required pausing six weeks of feature development and rebuilding from a different premise. The research made the case clearly.

I ran a two-day alignment workshop with the founding team to pressure-test the new direction and define what success would look like for the MVP.

The central metaphor was a spatial canvas — a day-by-day grid where users drag and drop saved places, add personal notes, and see their itinerary take shape visually. Unlike a list-based planner, the canvas gives a sense of spatial rhythm: mornings, afternoons, evenings, travel time.

Progressive Preferences

Rather than a lengthy onboarding quiz, contextual tags appear the first time a user saves a place. Implicit signals improve recommendations over time without upfront investment.

04

Validation & Testing

Proving the hypothesis. Four rounds of prototype testing on the canvas interaction model, iterating until drag-and-drop felt genuinely fluid.

Usability Score 89% Task Completion Rate
Retention 2.8× 90-day retention (preference users)
The Feedback Loop

Testing isn't a final hurdle; it's a recurring check-in. The canvas drag-and-drop went through four major iterations before it passed unmoderated testing without a single recovery behaviour observed.
