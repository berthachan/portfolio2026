The Sweet Spot  /
The "Five Whys" Framework

I use iterative questioning to drill down past superficial features into core functional and emotional needs. It takes discipline to stop at the right level of abstraction.

Nexus had grown through acquisition, inheriting three separate frontend codebases. Engineers were spending 30% of sprint capacity resolving design inconsistencies instead of shipping features.

I ran 14 contextual interviews across London, New York, and Singapore — observational sessions where I watched users work in their actual environment.

The biggest finding: the primary usability issue wasn't the interface — it was the information architecture. Menus were labelled from an engineering perspective, not a trading workflow perspective.

I synthesised findings into three user archetypes and a revised task hierarchy that became the north star for the entire redesign.

I architected the design system using a three-tier token structure: primitive → semantic → component. This meant theming (light/dark) required changing a single token layer, and the entire system updated automatically.

The data visualisation library was the most complex undertaking — 26 chart variants with consistent interaction patterns, tooltip systems, and colour-blind safe palettes.
