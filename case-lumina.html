Lumina Health Dashboard

Designing a patient-facing chronic illness management dashboard that translates complex clinical data into actionable, emotionally considered insights.

RoleUX Lead & Researcher
Duration7 Months
PlatformWeb (Desktop-first)
ToolsFigma, Dovetail, D3.js Prototypes
RecognitionHealthcare UX Award 2022

01 — Context

Data without meaning is noise

Lumina provides a remote monitoring platform for patients managing chronic conditions — primarily Type 2 diabetes and hypertension. Clinicians could see rich dashboards; patients saw a stripped-down mobile screen with numbers and no context.

Elena Rodriguez, Design Director at Lumina, brought me in to design a patient-facing dashboard that could sit alongside the clinical view — giving patients genuine insight without the cognitive overwhelm of raw medical data.

The unique challenge here was emotional: this data is deeply personal, and presenting it badly could cause anxiety or false reassurance. Every design decision had to be considered through both a usability lens and an emotional one.

I worked in close collaboration with two clinical advisors throughout the project to ensure medical accuracy and appropriate framing of all health trends.

02 — Research

Listening before designing

I conducted 22 in-depth interviews with patients across different literacy levels, ages, and conditions. The sessions were deliberately slow-paced, with open-ended prompts: "Tell me about a time you felt confused by your health data." The responses were consistent: numbers felt threatening, graphs felt clinical, and most patients defaulted to asking their GP what the data meant rather than interpreting it themselves.

I also ran a card sort with 16 participants to understand how patients naturally grouped health concepts — which led to a revised information architecture that matched patient mental models rather than clinical categories.

All research was synthesised in Dovetail, and I produced a 34-page research report that became a foundational document for the product and clinical teams.

03 — Design Principles

Three principles that governed every decision

01Context over numbersEvery metric shown in relation to a goal or trend, never in isolation.
02Calm confidenceVisual language chosen to reduce anxiety: soft colour ramps, plain language labels, no alarming red states.
03Action over reportingEvery insight paired with a suggested next step the patient can actually take.

04 — Outcomes

Impact across patients and clinicians

The dashboard launched to a beta cohort of 800 patients. In the first three months, self-reported understanding of personal health data rose from 34% to 71%. Clinical teams reported a meaningful reduction in follow-up calls from patients seeking explanations of their data.

The project received the Healthcare UX Award for 2022 in the Patient Experience category. Elena Rodriguez's testimonial — "Rarely do you find a designer who understands both the deep aesthetic nuances of typography and the hardcore logic of data structures" — remains one of the pieces of feedback I'm most proud of.
