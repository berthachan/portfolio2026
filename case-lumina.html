Discovery & Alignment

Every project starts with radical honesty. For Lumina, that meant confronting a difficult truth: data without emotional context can cause more harm than it prevents.

The Sweet Spot  /
Research Insight

In 22 interviews, patients consistently said numbers felt threatening and graphs felt clinical. Most defaulted to asking their GP what the data meant, rather than interpreting it themselves.

I conducted 22 in-depth interviews with patients across different literacy levels, ages, and conditions. Sessions were deliberately slow-paced with open-ended prompts: "Tell me about a time you felt confused by your health data."

I also ran a card sort with 16 participants to understand how patients naturally grouped health concepts — leading to a revised IA that matched patient mental models rather than clinical categories.

Every design decision was reviewed through both a usability lens and an emotional one. I worked with two clinical advisors throughout to ensure medical accuracy and appropriate framing of all health trends.

All research was synthesised in Dovetail, producing a 34-page report that became a foundational document for product and clinical teams.

Three principles governed every architectural decision: context over numbers (every metric in relation to a goal), calm confidence (soft colour ramps, plain language, no alarming red states), and action over reporting (every insight paired with a suggested next step).

Interaction & Visuals

Building the high-fidelity interface with a calm, considered visual language. Accessibility and emotional safety were the primary constraints.
